Getting
Started

Everything you need to know about our medications, how they work, and how to begin your care journey at Weight Loss Davie.

What are semaglutide and tirzepatide, and how do they work?

Semaglutide and tirzepatide are prescription medications used in certain patients for blood sugar management and, in some cases, weight management when prescribed by a licensed healthcare provider. These medications affect hormones involved in appetite and blood sugar regulation. Eligibility and treatment decisions are made during a medical evaluation.

How do I get started?

Call us at 310.408.6687 or visit WeightLossDavie.com to begin the intake process. You will complete a patient profile and schedule a consultation with a licensed provider to review your medical history, goals, and treatment options.

What makes Weight Loss Davie different?

Weight Loss Davie provides provider-led, individualized weight management care with direct communication, follow-up visits, and treatment planning based on your medical history, progress, and safety considerations.

What is WeightLossDavie.com?

WeightLossDavie.com is the website for our medical weight management practice in Davie, Florida. It provides information about our services, consultations, eligibility review, and follow-up care.

How does the Weight Loss Davie program work?

The program begins with an initial consultation and medical review. If appropriate, your provider develops an individualized care plan. Follow-up visits and ongoing monitoring are used to review progress, adjust care, and support long-term health goals.

Do I need insurance to begin care?

No. We offer self-pay options, and insurance is not required to begin an evaluation.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept approved payment methods, including cash and electronic payment options. Please contact our office for the current list of accepted payment methods.

Is there a long-term contract or membership requirement?

There is no long-term membership contract. Treatment timelines vary by patient. Any decision to start, pause, or stop treatment should be discussed with your provider as part of your care plan.

Who is a candidate for these treatments?

Candidates are typically adults who meet clinical criteria for weight management treatment. Eligibility is determined by a licensed provider based on BMI, medical history, current conditions, medications, and overall health status.

How much weight can I expect to lose?

Weight loss outcomes vary by individual. Your provider will discuss realistic expectations based on your medical history, treatment plan, nutrition, activity level, and follow-up adherence.

Questions 11–15

Treatment &
Side Effects

Understand how medications are administered, common side effects, treatment duration, and what happens when care changes over time.

How are these medications administered?

If prescribed, these medications are typically administered as a once-weekly subcutaneous injection. Your provider will review instructions, proper use, and safety guidance.

What are common side effects?

Side effects vary by patient and medication. Some people experience gastrointestinal symptoms such as nausea, diarrhea, or constipation. Your provider will review potential side effects, risk factors, and when to seek medical care.

How long will I need to stay on medication?

Treatment duration varies based on your health history, response to treatment, goals, and provider recommendations. Your care plan is reviewed over time and adjusted as needed.

What happens if I stop the medication?

Changes in appetite, weight, or blood sugar may occur after stopping medication. Your provider can help you plan next steps and discuss nutrition, activity, and follow-up strategies to support long-term health goals.

Can I use these medications if I have diabetes?

Some medications used in weight management were originally developed for blood sugar management in certain patients. Whether a medication is appropriate depends on your diagnosis, medical history, and provider evaluation.

What kind of support will I receive?

Patients receive provider-led guidance, follow-up visits, progress reviews, and individualized counseling on treatment adherence and lifestyle habits as part of their care plan.

Do I need to follow a specific diet or exercise plan?

Your provider will discuss nutrition and activity recommendations based on your health history and goals. Lifestyle changes are often an important part of weight management and ongoing care.

Are these treatments covered by insurance?

Insurance coverage varies by plan, diagnosis, and treatment. We offer self-pay options for patients who prefer or require out-of-pocket care. Contact our office for current pricing information.

What should I do to prepare for treatment?

You may be asked to complete a medical history form, provide current medications, and share recent lab work or other records if available. Your provider will review what is needed during the consultation.

Are there contraindications or reasons someone should not use these medications?

These medications are not appropriate for everyone. Certain medical conditions, medication interactions, or health histories may affect eligibility. A licensed provider will review your medical history and determine whether treatment is appropriate and safe for you.
